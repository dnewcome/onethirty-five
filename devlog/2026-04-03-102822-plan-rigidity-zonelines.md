## What happened

Did a deep planning pass on two connected problems: how to visualize the continuous helical zone lines that wind around the dome surface (each generator defines one helix), and how to think about the structural rigidity of the zonohedron as a physical build. The rigidity analysis was surprisingly interesting — a polar zonohedron made of poles and pin joints has N(N−1) internal mechanisms (every rhombic face can shear), and Maxwell's rule makes the deficit precise. The plan lays out triangulation options, flat-strip fabrication feasibility, and the three-way weave route as the most physically achievable path to a rigid woven dome.
