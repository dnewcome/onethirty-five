# Initial commit: phyllotaxis and zome visualizers

_2026-04-03_

## What happened

Started the project with two complete single-file visualizers. The phyllotaxis tool grew out of wanting to understand why sunflowers, pinecones, and pineapples all express the same Fibonacci spiral structure — the answer is the 137.5° golden angle, and the simulator makes it viscerally clear by letting you drag the angle away from that value and watch the spirals collapse. The zome explorer came from realizing that the same golden-angle geometry governs polar zonohedra, the mathematical basis of Zome dome structures. Both tools are self-contained HTML files with no build step.
